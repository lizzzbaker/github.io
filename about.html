Hi, I’m Elizabeth.

I design learning experiences, interfaces, and educational tools that help children think more clearly and creatively in a world shaped by technology. I’m especially interested in how curiosity, attention, and early cognitive habits develop, and how well-designed environments can support a wide range of learners.

My work blends learning sciences, human–computer interaction, and curriculum design. I care deeply about making educational tools feel intuitive, warm, and accessible— especially for neurodivergent learners whose needs are often ignored or oversimplified.

This portfolio is a place where I explore ideas, share prototypes, and build small experiments that reflect the questions I’m currently pursuing as I prepare for doctoral study.
