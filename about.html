About Me

I am a designer and researcher focused on how children think, learn, and make sense of the world. My work blends learning sciences, child development, human–computer interaction, and curriculum design.

I graduated from the University of Michigan — Ann Arbor with a B.S. in Computer Science and a minor in Mathematics. I’ve taught and tutored learners across ages, and I care deeply about creating technology and curriculum that feel intuitive, warm, and cognitively supportive — especially for neurodivergent students.

My interests include early cognitive skill development, metacognition, creativity in learning environments, and playful computational tools. I’m preparing for doctoral study, where I hope to explore how educational technologies can better support curiosity and equitable opportunities for young learners.
