Hi — I’m Elizabeth.

I design playful, research-grounded tools for how children think, learn, and create in an increasingly technological world. I’m especially drawn to the cognitive texture of childhood — curiosity, confusion, metacognition, attention, frustration, imagination — and how learning environments can shape those experiences.

My work sits at the intersection of learning sciences, HCI for education, early-childhood curriculum design, and neurodivergent-informed approaches.

This portfolio is a collection of small experiments: prototypes, interfaces, classroom-friendly tools, writing, research notes, and questions I’m following as I prepare for doctoral study.
