About

Research stance, preparation, and goals.

Research Agenda

I study how AI can amplify—not replace—children’s thinking. I focus on metacognition, curiosity, and transfer in K-5 contexts, designing tools and curricula that scaffold reflection, self-explanation, and productive struggle.

Methodological Approach

  • Design-based research and classroom prototyping
  • Mixed methods: qualitative observation + lightweight analytics
  • Co-design with teachers; ethics-forward HCI practices

Academic Preparation

  • B.S. Computer Science, minor in Mathematics
  • Research projects in edtech, visualization, and human-centered design
  • Teaching & mentoring in K-5 and undergraduate contexts

Future Goals

  • Ph.D. in Learning Sciences / Educational Technology
  • Lead research on AI-supported metacognition in early grades
  • Build teacher-friendly open resources and PD
